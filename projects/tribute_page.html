Patrice Bergeron
Boston's latest hockey hero
Patrice Bergeron celebrates with his teammates after scoring a goal.
Here is a timeline of Patrice Bergeron's hockey career:
  • 2003 - Drafted in the 2nd Round (45th overall) by the Boston Bruins, scores 16 goals, 23 assists, for a total of 39 points in 71 games
  • 2004 - Wins the gold medal in the World Championships as a member of Canada
  • 2004-2005 Season - Plays the entire year for the Providence Bruins due to the NHL lockout
  • 2005 - Wins the gold medal in the World Junior Championships as a member of Canada
  • 2005-2006 Season - Scores 31 goals, 42 assists for a total of 73 points, in 81 games
  • 2006-2007 Season - Scores 22 goals, 48 assists for a total of 70 points, in 77 games
  • 2007-2008 Season - 10 games into his season, it is ended with a severe concussion as a result of a violent hit
  • 2008-2009 Season - Returns from injury to score 8 goals, 31 assists for a total of 39 points in 64 games
  • 2009-2010 Season - Scores 19 goals, 33 assits for a total of 52 points in 73 games
  • 2010 - Wins the gold medal in the 2010 Olympics as a member of team Canada
  • 2010-2011 Season - Scores 22 goals, 35 assissts for a total of 57 points in 80 games, en route to winning the Stanley Cup
  • 2011-2012 Season - Scores 22 goals, 42 assists for a total of 64 points in 81 games
  • 2013 Season - Season is delayed until January 2013 due to another NHL lockout, scores 10 goals, 22 assists for a total of 32 points in 42 games
  • 2013-2014 Season - Scores 30 goals, 32 assists for a total of 62 points in 80 games
  • 2014 - Wins the Gold Medal in the 2014 Olympics as a member of team Canada
  • 2014-2015 Season - Scores 23 goals, 32 assists for a total of 55 points in 81 games
  • 2015-2016 Season - Scores 32 goals, 36 assists for a total of 68 points in 80 games
  • 2016 - Wins the Gold Medal at the World Cup of Hockey as a member of Team Canada
  • 2016-2017 Season - Scores 21 goals, 32 assists for a total of 53 points in 79 games
